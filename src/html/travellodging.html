Travel

Fly Seattle-Tacoma (SEA) Airport
Car It is easier to travel through the Seattle area with a car, so we recommend either renting a car or carpooling with other guests.

The cost to rent a car from ENTERPRISE, BUDGET, ALAMO, or AVIS from the airport is about ~$30/day including taxes if you book in advance online at carrentals.com, rentalcars.com, or priceline.com.

If you are not renting a car, please note that UBER and LYFT cannot pick up passengers at the airport. You can take a taxi straight to your lodging or take a quick taxi ride to another area and order an uber or lyft pickup from there.

Lodging

We recommend finding lodgings through Airbnb or staying with us at Salish Lodge & Spa.

Airbnb

The most cost-effective lodgings are with Airbnb. Large Airbnb homes with multiple bedrooms and bathrooms can be split between multiple wedding guests with rates as low as $70/night in Seattle. If you've never used Airbnb before, our referral link will give you a $30 discount (and will give us $30 off our next stay, too!). airbnb.com/c/nancyt61

Seattle is approximately 30 minutes away from the airport and a 35 minute drive to Salish Lodge for the wedding. Airbnb rentals for private rooms and entire homes start from $80/night with most listings within $90-$160/night.

Issaquah is a 15 minute drive to Salish Lodge, a 25 minute drive from the airport, and 20 minutes away from Seattle. Airbnb listings begin at $80/night.

There are also numerous Airbnb listings in Bellevue and Sammamish, which are located between Seattle and Issaquah.

Salish Lodge & Spa

Salish Lodge is a 45 minute drive away from the airport. All guestrooms have wood burning fireplaces, large two-person jetted spa tubs, and either a king sized or two double featherbeds. salishlodge.com/rooms.php

To book a guestroom, call reservations at 1-800-272-5474. Please mention you are with the Tien-Wilson Wedding or reservation #45V885 for a discount. The lodge is pricey at about $320/night including taxes and the discount.

About

Salish Lodge is a Native-American owned historic luxury hotel. The Muckleshoot Tribe purchased Salish Lodge & Spa in 2007, using profits to reacquire lands surrounding their reservation, preserve ancient salmon runs, and support community impact non-profit organizations involved in education, health and human services, the arts, civic/community advocacy for people of color, and environmental programs. In 2013, they bought back 150 square miles of forest surrounding their reservation in Washington state.
